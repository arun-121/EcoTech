some common instructions to be followed


1 Wear protective clothing:
Before you begin disassembling e-waste, you should put on protective clothing such as gloves, goggles, and a face mask to protect yourself from harmful substances.


2 Disconnect from power source:
Make sure the device is disconnected from any power source, such as a battery or electrical outlet, before starting the disassembly process.


3 Sort components:
Sort the components of the e-waste into different categories such as metal, plastic, glass, and circuit boards. This makes it easier to recycle or dispose of each component properly.


4 Use proper tools:
Use proper tools such as screwdrivers, pliers, and cutters to remove components. Avoid using tools that could damage components or cause injury.


5 Dispose of hazardous materials:
Some components of e-waste contain hazardous materials such as lead and mercury. Make sure to dispose of these materials properly according to local regulations.


6 Recycle or dispose of components:
After disassembling the e-waste, the components should be either recycled or disposed of properly according to local regulations.
